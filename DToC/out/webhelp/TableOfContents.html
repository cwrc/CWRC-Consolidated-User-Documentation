Table of contents

The Table of Contents shows the section or chapter breakdown of your document. The ToC will expand to display the information selected from the left column.

Each section is represented by an orange dot and the title(s) and author(s) specified in your XML document. By clicking on one of the sections or chapters you change what appears in the reading column to what you’ve selected.

If you paste plain text or type in an URL in the DToC homepage there will not be anything in the Table of Contents.



When you select an option from the “Index, Tab, Stats or Annotation” column the Table of Contents will display and link to the location of the term/tag /word.



You can search the “Table of Contents.” The search box is auto complete enabled, but partial matches are not recognized (you cannot just type in the first few letters of a word and press enter as that will not be recognized). Once a search is conducted, the different sections or chapters that contain the search term will expand and show where in the text they are located with brief excerpts. To search for a new term, delete your word in the “Word Search” box and type/select a new one or press the “Clear” button located at the top of the document model (to the right of the Table of Contents).
