      • Exploring DToC
        • Navigating text in the table of contents

          The Table of Contents displays terms selected from the left column, and lets users move through the text by clicking on them. This allows for in-depth exploration of terms without the trouble of scrolling through the corpus manually.

        • Navigating the document model

          The Document Model is a deconstructed version of your document. Each section of grey lines represents a different section, chapter, or article within the document.

        • Navigating the Reading Pane

          The text in the Reading Pane is separated into chapters, sections, or articles by the XML document. The Reading Pane loads individual sections; the entire document will not appear unless no text divisions have been embedded into the document.

        • Filtering index terms, tags, and stats

          The Search feature narrows down terms automatically as you type. The string of characters being entered into into the Search bar will bring up words containing that string, regardless of whether the word begins with those characters or not.

        • Searching the full text

          The search box is autocomplete-enabled.

        • Filtering by chapter

          When you move between different tabs in the left column they will not be filtered by the Chapter you have selected in the previous column. However, when you return to the initial tab, the chapter filter will remain selected.

        • Sorting word frequencies

          You can customize how you view word frequencies in DToC by different organizational headings and by how the words are displayed under those headings.

Favoriting words

You can add as many terms as you would like to your “Favorites” list, either all at once or a few at a time.

  1. Select words you would like to favorite by checking the box beside them.

  2. Click on the double black arrow icon located at the lower right corner of the “Stats” column.
  3. Click on the red heart icon that has the green plus sign. This will add the selected terms to a “Favorites” list.



  4. To switch back and forth from the original list to your “favorites,” click on the heart icon (that does not have any additional icons attached).

  5. To remove a word from your “Favorites” list, select it and click on the heart that has a red minus icon. This icon is not located in the favorites drop down menu, but beside it.
